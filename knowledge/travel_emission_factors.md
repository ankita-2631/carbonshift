# Travel Emission Factors — Reference

Cite this document as "Travel Emission Factors". Figures are typical UK averages used
for decision support; they are estimates, not a live data feed.

## Carbon per passenger-kilometre (gCO2e/km)
- Petrol car: ~170 g per vehicle-km (shared across occupants).
- Electric car: ~47 g per vehicle-km (depends on grid mix when charged).
- Rail (national): ~35 g per passenger-km.
- Virtual meeting (video call): negligible, ~0.1 g per participant-km equivalent.

## How CarbonShift compares trips
- Car emissions are per-vehicle, so car-pooling lowers the per-person figure.
- Rail and virtual are counted per person.
- Distance is doubled for return trips.
- The lowest-carbon mode that respects the trip's constraints is recommended.

## Cost per kilometre (indicative, GBP)
- Petrol car: ~£0.25/km (fuel + wear).
- Electric car: ~£0.10/km.
- Rail: ~£0.18/km per ticket.
- Virtual: £0.

## Constraints CarbonShift always respects
- An "essential" trip (must be attended in person) is never replaced by a virtual
  meeting; only the travel mode may change.
- Recommendations are advisory; the traveller makes the final call.

## Honesty and limits
- Real emissions vary with vehicle, occupancy, route, and electricity mix.
- Use these as directional estimates for prioritising the biggest, easiest wins.
